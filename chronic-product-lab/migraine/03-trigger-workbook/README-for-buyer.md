# Migraine Pattern Workbook

Thank you for using this printable.

This 10-page workbook is designed to help you organize possible context around migraine attacks, review patterns over time, and prepare clearer notes for a healthcare appointment.

## How to Use

1. Print the full workbook, or print only the pages you need.
2. Use the attack log for migraine days or individual attacks.
3. Use the context pages to record sleep, schedule, food, caffeine, hydration, alcohol, stress, sensory exposure, weather, cycle/body notes, and medication/support notes.
4. Write "unknown," "not sure," or "no clear pattern" when that is the honest answer.
5. Use the final review page to summarize what showed up more than once and what you want to ask your healthcare provider.
6. Skip any section that is not relevant to you.

## A Note on Possible Triggers

Migraine patterns can be complicated. This workbook is for collecting clues, not proving a cause. Some possible triggers may actually be early migraine symptoms, multiple factors may stack together, and some attacks may not have an obvious pattern.

This is not about blaming yourself.

## Printing Tips

- Print at 100% scale when possible.
- Black-and-white printing is fine.
- If anything prints too close to the edge, choose "Fit to printable area" in your printer settings.
- Use a folder, binder, or clipboard to keep the pages together.
- Reprint individual pages as needed.

## Important Medical Note

This printable is a personal tracking and organization tool. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always talk with a qualified healthcare professional about symptoms, medications, or care decisions.

This workbook can help organize possible context around migraine attacks. It cannot prove what caused an attack, and it should not be used to make medication, diet, or care changes without a qualified healthcare professional.

If symptoms feel new, sudden, severe, or concerning, contact a qualified healthcare professional or emergency services.
