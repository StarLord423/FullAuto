# Critique: Migraine Pattern Workbook

## Final Verdict

Pass.

This product is good enough for local validation as a 10-page US Letter migraine pattern workbook. It is migraine-specific, medically cautious, printable, and useful for organizing possible context without promising to identify causes or prevent attacks. The pre-Critic layout pass reduced the large bottom gaps by adding page-specific review areas while preserving the approved meaning, page count, safety wording, and scope.

## Top 5 Issues

1. The product is strong enough to pass, but marketplace preview mockups still need to be created before any listing work.
2. The visual identity is intentionally restrained; future shop branding could make it feel more premium without changing the worksheet content.
3. A4 is now included and verified; marketplace preview images still need to show both formats clearly.
4. The workbook covers one structured review cycle. Some buyers may want duplicate weekly log pages or a longer tracking bundle later.
5. The title uses "trigger" for search visibility, so listing copy must continue pairing it with cautious language about possible context and uncertainty.

## Required Revisions

None for local validation.

## Optional Improvements

- Add branded cover/mockup images during the listing asset step.
- Keep both US Letter and A4 PDFs in the final upload package.
- Consider a future expanded version with duplicate weekly pages for buyers who want longer tracking.
- Add a small shop-brand footer only if it does not crowd handwriting space.

## Marketplace Risk Notes

Avoid claims such as:

- "find your triggers"
- "identify the cause"
- "prevent migraines"
- "control your migraines"
- "eliminate triggers"
- "doctor-approved"
- "migraine cure"

Safer positioning:

- "organize possible migraine context"
- "review patterns over time"
- "collect notes for a healthcare conversation"
- "track clues without assuming one clear cause"
- "printable migraine workbook"

The product should continue to make clear that it is a personal tracking and organization tool, not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
