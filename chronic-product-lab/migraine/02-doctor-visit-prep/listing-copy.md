## Description

Prepare for a migraine-related appointment with a printable pack designed to help you summarize what is easy to forget once the visit starts: migraine frequency, symptom patterns, aura notes, possible context, medication and treatment history, daily-life impact, questions, and follow-up instructions.

This pack is made for primary care, neurology, headache specialist, and follow-up appointments. It is practical, low-ink, and handwriting-friendly, with migraine-specific prompts instead of generic appointment-planner pages.

## Who This Is For

This printable is for people with migraine or chronic migraine who want help preparing clear notes for a healthcare visit. It may be especially useful if appointments feel rushed, brain fog makes details hard to recall, or you want a concise way to explain how migraine affects work, school, caregiving, and daily life.

## What You Can Organize

- Appointment goals and top concerns
- Migraine days, headache days, aura days, and duration estimates
- Pain location, pain quality, aura, neurological symptoms, nausea, light sensitivity, sound sensitivity, smell sensitivity, fatigue, brain fog, and postdrome notes
- Possible context such as sleep changes, missed meals, caffeine changes, sensory exposure, weather, stress, travel, schedule disruption, and cycle timing
- Current and past medications or supports
- Side effects, barriers, refills, cost, or access concerns
- Impact on work, school, caregiving, transport, screen use, and daily tasks
- Questions for your provider
- Follow-up plan and next steps

## How to Use

1. Print the full pack before your appointment.
2. Fill in only the pages that match your visit.
3. Use short notes if symptoms, fatigue, or brain fog make writing difficult.
4. Bring completed migraine trackers, medication lists, or records if they are helpful.
5. Use the follow-up page during or right after the appointment so you remember the plan.

## Important
