# Critique: Migraine Doctor Visit Prep Pack

## Final Verdict

Pass.

The product is sellable for local validation as an 8-page migraine appointment prep pack. It is medically cautious, migraine-specific, and genuinely useful for someone who wants to avoid blanking during a short healthcare visit. It does not make diagnosis, treatment, cure, prevention, or guaranteed-outcome claims.

## Top 5 Issues

1. Visual identity is intentionally minimal. Add consistent shop styling only after the shop identity is chosen.
2. Page 5 medication tables are intentionally conservative, but buyers with long treatment histories may need to print duplicates.
3. A4 is now included and verified; marketplace preview images still need to show both formats clearly.
4. Marketplace preview images will still need polish before publishing.
5. Some pages still leave small lower margins after the page label, but the large framed blank areas have been reduced.

## Required Revisions

- None required for local validation.

## Optional Improvements

- Add a light branded footer once the shop identity is chosen.
- Keep both US Letter and A4 PDFs in the final upload package.
- Add a separate summary-only page for buyers who want a one-page handout to give the provider.

## Marketplace Risk Notes

Medical risk is low. The product repeatedly frames itself as a personal organization and communication tool, not medical advice.

Avoid future listing phrases like:

- "Find your migraine triggers"
- "Prevent migraines"
- "Reduce migraine attacks"
- "Doctor-approved"
- "Know what treatment you need"

Safer phrases:

- "Organize possible context"
- "Prepare notes for your healthcare provider"
- "Summarize symptoms and impact"
- "Record questions and follow-up notes"
