## Short Hook

## Description

Organize migraine notes in one printable bundle made for real appointment prep and pattern review. This bundle includes three migraine-specific PDF products: a quick daily symptom tracker, a doctor visit prep pack, and a pattern workbook for reviewing possible context over time.

Use the tracker for individual migraine attacks, the workbook for noticing repeated patterns or uncertainty, and the appointment pack when you want clearer notes for a primary care, neurology, headache specialist, or follow-up visit.

The pages are designed to be practical, low-ink, handwriting-friendly, and migraine-specific. They include prompts for aura, symptoms, pain patterns, possible context, medication/support notes, daily-life impact, questions, and follow-up planning.

- 10-page Migraine Pattern Workbook
- US Letter PDF versions of all three products
- A4 PDF versions of all three products
- Bundle README with printing and safety notes

## Page Counts

- Doctor Visit Prep Pack: 8 pages
- Pattern Workbook: 10 pages
- Total printable pages per paper size: 20 pages

## Who This Is For

This bundle is for people with recurring migraine or chronic migraine who want a printable way to record attacks, organize appointment notes, and review possible patterns without relying on a phone app during pain, light sensitivity, or brain fog.

It may be especially helpful if appointments feel rushed, details are hard to remember later, or you want a clearer way to explain migraine frequency, symptoms, supports used, and daily-life impact.

## What You Can Track and Organize

- Migraine attack timing, duration, intensity, and impact
- Aura, neurological symptoms, nausea, light sensitivity, sound sensitivity, smell sensitivity, fatigue, brain fog, and postdrome notes
- Pain location and pain quality
- Possible context such as sleep changes, missed meals, hydration, caffeine, alcohol, sensory exposure, weather, stress, travel, schedule disruption, and cycle/body timing
- Medication/support timing, relief, side effects, and access concerns
- Migraine frequency, headache days, aura days, and appointment priorities
- Questions for a healthcare provider
- Follow-up notes and next steps
- Patterns that appeared more than once
- Areas where there is no clear pattern or not enough information yet

## How to Use

1. Print the Daily Symptom Tracker when you want to record one migraine attack.
2. Use the Pattern Workbook when you want to review several entries or possible context notes over time.
3. Fill out the Doctor Visit Prep Pack before an appointment, using only the pages that match your visit.
4. Bring completed pages to a healthcare appointment if they are useful.
5. Reprint individual pages whenever you need more copies.

## Important

This is a digital printable bundle. No physical item will be shipped.

These printables are personal tracking and organization tools. They are not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always talk with a qualified healthcare professional about symptoms, medications, or care decisions.

This bundle can help organize possible context around migraine attacks. It cannot prove what caused an attack, and it should not be used to make medication, diet, or care changes without a qualified healthcare professional.

If symptoms feel new, sudden, severe, or concerning, contact a qualified healthcare professional or emergency services.
